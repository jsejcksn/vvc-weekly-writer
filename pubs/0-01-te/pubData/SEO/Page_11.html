Classroom © 2015 Virtual Village Classroom © 2015 Virtual Village Classroom © ASSESSMENT Children want to write I see, I hear, I smell, I taste, I touch, I feel, I like, and I love. This is very basic, and there is nothing wrong with it. However, from this point, there needs to be a progression to something more substantial. We need to create stronger sentences that infer seeing, hearing, smelling, tasting, touching, feeling, liking, and loving without using those words all the time. In the Apply It lesson, we will be focusing on seeing and hearing. In whole group, write a new sentence that infers seeing or hearing. Then, revise the sentence (stretch it) to include juicy descriptive words. Utilize observation assessment to determine the level of understanding.
