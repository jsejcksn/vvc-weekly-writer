Village Classroom © 2015 Virtual Village Classroom © 2015 Virtual What is descriptive? Descriptive writing paints pictures with words. When we describe, we let the reader experience something. It may be a thing, a place, a person, or something that happens. To describe, we need to ask questions. Let’s use our senses to ask great questions: • What do you see? • What do you hear? • What do you smell? • What do you taste? • How does it feel to touch? • How does it make you feel inside? The last question pulls on our emotions. Writing about how we feel gives great voice and expression to our writing. To learn more about writing in the descriptive mode, watch the writing lesson with Mr. Butler and RIP! In the video, Mr. Butler will walk you through the writing process of brainstorming, planning, writing, and revising and editing.
